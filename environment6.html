Education & Farming that empowers people and heals the Earth



Rs 4,841,484



Built in midst of Sahara desert, supports the production of dates and raisins



Who we are


We, Anna Maria and Lucian, grew up in families rooted in homesteading, farming, and stewardship of land and community. We were raised to love, respect and live in harmony with nature and all the people we encountered. We pursued higher education to better understand how we can continue this legacy and chose careers to do so also. For the past 10 years we have been working in the realms of sustainability, permaculture and regenerative work in academic, government, business and nonprofit environments. We designed, developed and implemented programs and projects that enhanced and continue to enhance the lives of thousands of people, and the health of numerous ecosystems. We are designers, educators, farmers, healers, stewards and regenerators of nature and communities.



Our Mission.


Throughout our lives and careers we experienced the health, abundance and joy regenerative and sustainability based lifestyles and practices can bring to people, communities and all life in general. We have also experienced and learned about the opposite when those practices are missing from people .
Knowing this, early in our adulthood, we committed to spread awareness and help people learn and integrate practical regenerative and sustainability based solutions into their personal and professional lives. The results we helped create gave us a strong sense of purpose and we knew that this is the work we want to do for the rest of our lives: to be in service to all people, the environment, the common future of us all, and life in general. Our mission has been and continues to be: to inspire, empower and support a regenerative and sustainability based future.



Desert Blooms.


To expand our impact, to restore more degraded and sensitive ecosystems, to grow more nutritious food and medicine and share the surplus, and to inspire, empower and support more people to take on this journey for the benefit of all, we relocated to a 40 acre property in the arid high desert of Anza. We chose this space because it is a sensitive ecosystem, degraded and endangered, and has immense potential for regeneration. In a very short time, starting in July 2019, we renovated the house, produced lots of veggies and medicinal herbs from a small garden, planted fruit trees and shrubs, built a chicken coop for our 8 rescued chicks, started on some habitat restoration projects, hosted multiple work parties, a leadership course and a permaculture design certification course. .



Our plans with Desert Bloom .


Our plan, already in action, is to transform this degraded space into a working Permaculture and Regenerative Research and Education Farm. We planned it as a combination of entrepreneurial and non-profit endeavors. In this space we will: 1.grow food and medicine with regenerative practices for ourselves, our apprentices, our students and the market,

2.educate and train people in permaculture design, regenerative farming, ecosystems restoration, beekeeping, herbalism, earth and people centered leadership and many other related subjects in these realms,

3.restore and enhance the farm sensitive ecosystems for long term resilience,

4.host interested individuals to experience the beauty of our work and the surrounding nature,

5.track and record all of our practices and progress and share them with the world.

We are committed to demonstrate that this work is possible, feasible, beautiful and with extensive benefits to all life, even when done from a harsh high desert arid environment. We will use this model and the surplus it produces to help other similar projects get off the ground.
