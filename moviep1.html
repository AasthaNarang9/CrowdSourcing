Dornalai Cailin : Girl Boxer Pilot Project



A hard-up-single-mother and former boxer, is lured by the Irish mob into an underground boxing match

Rs 1,294,474,



We are making something big and we are not looking back.





Overview

    I have always been fascinated by the underworld.
    the darker side of life where things happen unknown to the mass majority of people.
    I am, however, an optimist in that I believe most people are good.
    If this is the case, what leads people to do bad things?
    The story of Dornalai Cailin -Gaelic for Girl Boxer- explores this very question.
    We are looking to fund the creation of the pilot/short for the greater story that needs telling.
    All people need to make decisions as to which path they take.








About the Project

This project separates itself from others in its attention to detail.
The truth is in these details. We have a boxing instructor as a fight choreographer
and a Northern Irish filmmaker who has been instrumental in assuring both language and history is on point.
Our actors are training with dialect coaches to get specifics just right.

Having made these connections, our story line leads us forward
and we have the foundation in place to take this pilot out of the US and into Ireland and the Irish market.

In supporting this film, you are supporting WORKING WOMEN
- MOTHERS who are keeping their families together and trying to create at the same time.
Dornalai Cailin highlights just that, the FIGHT FOR FAMILY and the choices we must make while trying to maintain our morals.
This is the story of a woman who finds herself in the throws of the underworld...

Our cast and crew have won at Sundance (Jack Mulcahy: Brothers McMullen),
acted in Oscar nominated films (Emma Sara: Incendie),
graced the Broadway Stage (Matty Oaks: "Angles in America", David Sitler Frost/Nixon),
worked directly with ARNOLD (Brett Azar: Terminator Genysis)
and appeared in blockbuster network shows such as Blue Bloods, Sex and the City, Law and Order, Black List, Girls, Gone, Veep, Evil Lives Here, and Homicide:Life on the Street.
