A Good Ruckus - Pilot Episode



You can sit around, bored. Or you can create a ruckus.

5,00,000



It is first-year teacher Carole Franklin turn to watch over the kids in detention.
Upon everyone arriving, she notices an uncanny resemblance between her detention students and those that attended: The Breakfast Club.
This is the prequel to Ashs script: A Good Ruckus











Why are we here?

    Hi! I am Ash Willeby, the writer.
    While I went through college, I worked on film sets, small and large. I have held many positions, including- Production Assistant, Craft Services, Second A.D., Prop Master, Set Decorator, Art Director, and Production Designer.
    After getting my Masters in Psychology, I decided my skills and temperament were better suited to screenwriting. I write female-driven comedies, so I can see more stories I relate to get onto the big screen.
    You may have seen my numerous posts about my feature A GOOD RUCKUS collecting laurels. It is the story of Carole Franklin, a teacher in her fifth year who gets so bored of the daily grind that she invents some excitement for herself and her best friend.


Using Funds, To The Max!

What are the funds actually for?
We want to make a high-quality pilot for this web series. So, we want to hire skilled professionals to help this idea manifest.
Some of our talented friends, who do this for a living, have agreed to adjusted fee schedules, because they want to see this pilot made.
Not only will this pilot be the beginning of the Web Series: A GOOD RUCKUS, the footage can also be used to showcase the style and comedy of the award-winning feature-length script of the same name.

Location: To be able to film at the High School, with kids, we need to have stellar insurance. We will also need to cover any facility fees, including janitorial, permits, and for staff/admin to be present.
Lodging: Our High School location is a bit out of town. We'll need to have places for the crew to sleep after a long day of shooting. No driving accidents due to exhaustion!
Equipment: We will need to rent sound, grip, lighting and camera packages, to make sure filming goes smoothly.
Food: I, for one, get grumpy when I am hungry. No use having any hangry cast or crew. We will ensure everyone has plenty to eat on set, including those with dietary restrictions.
Post Production: We need this pilot to have that sweet 80s feel! Editing, Soundtrack, Graphics.. some of the things no one notices when they are done right.
