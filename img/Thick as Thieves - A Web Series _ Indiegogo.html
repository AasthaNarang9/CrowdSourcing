The Story

Adulthood. You've got to make it work right? Alisha, Dan, Jess and Temi are friends struggling with adulthood. They commit small time robberies in order to make ends meet.  One day they commit a crime that has consequences no one is ready for.

 

Who are we

We are The Basement Bunch, a collective of women working across theatre and film made up of team members, Adeyinka, Charity, Hannah and Shakira. We began The Basement Bunch as we wanted to create opportunities for others in our community and have since created theatre shows such as The Eden Effect and short films Nca and 4C. As we've grown we realised collaboration is at our core which is why we have teamed up with actresses Jessica and Mariah to create a web series.  We were intrigued by ensemble pieces and wanted create one of our own. We hope that this web series is a fun and exciting watch for the audience and leads to more collaborations in the future with other creatives.

To give you an idea of what the web series will look like, have a look at our teaser below.

 

 

So who will be involved?

The series will be directed by:

Shakira Francis 

Shakira directed her first short film '4C' that was selected as a finalist for the South East Film competition. It then went on to screen at the Cambridge Film Festival, the Brighton Cinecity Festival and tour the South East of England.  Her next short film 'The Lost Land Girl' was supported by the BFI and is currently making the festival circuit rounds. As a member of BFI network x Bafta Crew 2019, Shakira continues to write, direct and act

https://www.independentcinemaoffice.org.uk/film...
https://vimeo.com/350063403

Mariah Nonemacher

Mariah Nonnemacher a graduate with a Masters from the Royal Central School of Speech and Drama. Having directed documentaries in Shanghai for over a year with company LPfilms has started her career in Europe recent credits include La Vita, a documentary about refugees and locals finding common ground to find happiness in the small Italian village of Amantea. And La Visita, a short film LGBTQ thriller shot in Italy about life after coming out, and the trauma one endors even after accepting themselves. Now focused on the series of Thick as Thieves, a Luton based production as Director hopes to make it a truly ground breaking and unlike any other female driven crime series.

The impact you can help us make

As Women of Colour our representation both in front and behind the camera is our passion. This is why Thick as Thieves was developed. We wanted to showcase a story with WOC in the drivers seat with mutli-dimensional characters that are relatable. The industry is slowly changing with how it represents WOC but want to help it along a little bit further.  :)

Risks and Challenges

With filming there are always risks and challenges. From actors falling sick to the English weather not cooperating, However we have made sure we take in as many factors as possible when scheduling the film shoot. As a collective we have a wide range of production experience on multiple sets and have had to deal with cancellations of locations, clashes of personalities and unforeseen events on set.  We have overcome them and I am confident we can do the same with the web series, filming it on time and on budget.
